Energy begins at the cellular level.

Mitochondria help turn nutrients and oxygen into usable energy for movement, recovery, cognition, and metabolic function. When they work efficiently, the benefits can touch nearly every part of how we feel and perform.

SS-31, MOTS-c, and SLU-PP-332 are creating excitement because each speaks to a different part of the cellular energy story: protecting the membrane, strengthening the metabolic signal, and activating pathways connected to endurance and adaptation.

01 / Supporting the Membrane

SS-31: support where cellular energy begins.

SS-31, also known as elamipretide, is designed to work directly at the inner mitochondrial membrane. It interacts with cardiolipin, a key structural fat that helps keep the cell’s energy-producing machinery organized and efficient.

Elamipretide received FDA accelerated approval as Forzinity in 2025—a meaningful milestone for mitochondrial medicine. Its targeted mechanism makes SS-31 especially relevant to conversations about cellular energy, muscle function, stamina, and recovery.

02 / Supporting the Signal

MOTS-c: help cells respond, adapt, and communicate.

MOTS-c is a mitochondrial-derived peptide that acts like a metabolic messenger. It is connected to the way cells respond to exercise, energy demand, and metabolic stress—making it especially interesting for conversations about resilience, recovery, insulin sensitivity, and healthy aging.

Its metabolic relevance is easy to see: a current Phase 2a human study is focused on insulin sensitivity, glucose markers, lipids, body weight, and waist circumference in adults with early metabolic dysfunction.

03 / Supporting the Program

SLU-PP-332: activate the pathways behind metabolic performance.

SLU-PP-332 activates ERR pathways that help regulate mitochondrial growth, fat oxidation, cellular respiration, and the genetic program associated with aerobic exercise.

In preclinical research, it increased oxidative muscle fibers and exercise endurance. That combination explains the excitement around its connection to metabolic efficiency, muscle performance, and the way the body adapts to physical demand.
